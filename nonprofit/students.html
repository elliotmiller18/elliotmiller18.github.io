Students

What makes a student a good fit?
overview students course structure

We are looking for motivated students who already have some hands-on programming experience. This could come from AP Computer Science, robotics, personal projects, school assignments, online courses, or any other activity that has given them practice writing code. Our goal is to be an intro to advanced Computer Science topics that are typically inaccessible at the high school level, so an ideal student will have largely exhausted the programming resources offered at school.

This program is designed as an introduction to more advanced computer science topics that are often difficult to access at the high school level. An ideal student has already moved beyond the basics and is looking for a deeper understanding of how computers and software systems actually work.

Because the course will cover challenging material, students should be comfortable with basic programming concepts such as variables, conditionals, loops, functions, and simple data structures. They do not need to be experts, but they should be ready to learn independently and work through difficult ideas with support.

A solid academic standing is also helpful. We do not expect perfect grades, but students should be prepared to commit 1-2 hours a week outside of lessons. Consistent effort will be important for making progress.

Finally, it would be helpful if students could share a sample of programming work they have done, even if it is just a school assignment. This will help us better understand their current level and tailor lessons to their needs.
