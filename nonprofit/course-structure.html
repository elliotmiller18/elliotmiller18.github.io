Format

Instructors and students will meet once weekly after working hours (PST) or on a weekend at the student’s convenience. Lessons will be ~1 hour and will be one of:

  • A formal lecture explaining a concept
  • Pair coding sessions where students and instructors work on a material-related project together

Students are required to have a computer (including Chromebooks) to join meetings and develop on. While a personal computer is preferable, a school-managed computer will work as long as tutors or students have the ability to download required software.

The one-on-one format permits us a lot of flexibility in the structure of the course. Below is an informal schedule that is subject to change per student needs/wants.

Unit 1: Basics

1. Introductions + recap of CS fundamentals (Lecture)
  1. Homework: Set up development environment and do at least 1 Rustlings exercise
2. Rust basics (Lecture)
  1. Homework: Start on project 1 – Password Strength Checker
3. Reviewing project 1 (Pair Coding)
  1. Homework: write a couple sentences describing what you think a variable “is” before the next class

Unit 2: Memory

4. What really is a variable? (Lecture)
  1. Homework: Read about binary, bytes, addresses, and memory diagrams; complete a short worksheet
5. The stack vs the heap (Lecture)
  1. Homework: Read about stack frames, function calls, and heap allocation; draw memory diagrams for 2–3 small programs
6. Linked lists and malloc (Lecture)
  1. Homework: Read about linked lists and dynamic memory allocation in rust; answer conceptual questions about why arrays and linked lists behave differently
7. Project 2a – freelist (Pair Coding)
  1. Homework: finish project 2a
8. Project 2a review and intro to project 2b – malloc (Pair Coding and Lecture)
  1. Homework: work on project 2b
9. Project 2b review (Pair Coding)
  1. Homework: read up on different areas of systems and let us know which you want to explore after the class.
10. Dive into chosen topic (Discussion)
  1. Homework: look at the resources we provide and enjoy the rest of your summer!
