Our Goals
overview mission students course structure

There is an abundance of high quality, introductory programming material tailored to students of all ages.

However, as older or more advanced students try to deepen their knowledge, resources quickly dry up. Often, their only options are opaque technical documentation or tutorials targeted at undergraduate upperclassmen.

Our goal is to provide a deeper programming curriculum to advanced students with a demonstrated interest in programming/computers who are willing to commit 2-3 hours per week. (1 hour lesson on weeknights or weekends, 1-2 hours of independent work on projects)

My partner and I both work in specialized roles at frontier big tech companies (Apple, Amazon); these roles have high job security, carry a low risk of being replaced by AI, and have global impact.

Our curriculum would step beyond basic programming tutorials and be a project based introduction to the concepts that we use every day at work.

Along the way, students will develop an understanding of the principles behind the computer and software systems that solve the hardest and most important problems facing the world.

They will also get a feel for the structure and expectations of university computer science classes.
